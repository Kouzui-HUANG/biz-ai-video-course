## 1. Bone Structure & Contour (光影雕塑)

### Core English Terms
- `precision contouring` / `surgical contouring`
- `strategic highlighting` / `liquid highlight along the high planes`
- `facial architecture reconstruction`
- `pushed-back jawline shadow`
- `elevated cheekbone highlight`
- `narrowed nose-bridge contour` / `widened nose-bridge highlight`
- `adjusted facial-thirds proportion`
- `forehead-shortening shadow at the hairline`
- `chin-elongating highlight down the centerline`
- `temple-hollowing for an elongated face shape`

### Effect-Driven Phrasing
- To slim a wide face: *"cool-toned contour sculpting the perimeter of the cheek hollow, pulling the silhouette inward"*
- To lift drooping features: *"cream highlight feathered along the upper cheekbone arc, lifting the eye frame visually"*
- To balance a strong jaw: *"soft shadow tucked beneath the jawline angle, dissolved into the neck so the transition is invisible"*
- To shorten a long face: *"warm bronzer dusted at the lower hairline and under the chin, compressing vertical proportion"*

## 2. Complexion & Texture (底妝與膚質)

### Core English Terms
- `color-correcting base` (peach for under-eye blue, green for redness, lavender for sallow)
- `multi-tonal foundation` / `gradient base mixed two shades`
- `texture mixing` — luminous zones vs matte zones in one face
- `luminous apples of the cheeks`
- `matte T-zone control`
- `dewy lid finish`
- `flawless yet realistic skin texture` (NEVER "airbrushed perfection")
- `visible skin grain readable at close range`
- `subsurface glow / inner-lit complexion`

### Finish Vocabulary
- `satin finish` — between matte and dewy, soft sheen
- `glass skin glow` — Korean-style high-shine wet look
- `velvet matte` — powdery but soft, never flat or chalky
- `second-skin foundation` — barely-there, like skin itself

### Preserving Unique Traits
- `freckles intentionally preserved and visible through sheer base`
- `natural skin variation kept — no over-blurring`
- `birthmark left uncovered as a focal feature`
- `texture of the skin honored — pores and fine lines remain readable`

## 3. Brows & Eyes (眉眼靈魂)

### Brow Vocabulary
- `wild feathered brows` — natural, slightly unkempt, hair-by-hair definition
- `brushed-up brow gel finish` — boy-brow / soap-brow effect
- `bleached or blonded brows` — avant-garde editorial
- `architectural sharp brow` — high arch, clean edges
- `straight Korean-style brow` — low arch, soft taper

### Eye-Shape Engineering
- `fox-eye lift` — extended outer wing pulling shape upward and outward
- `doe-eye round` — soft kohl rim opening the eye downward and out
- `cut crease` — sharp transition between lid color and crease color
- `floating crease` — modern variant, color placed above the natural crease
- `siren eye / smoked siren wing` — elongated smoky outer
- `aegyo sal enhancement` — subtle highlight on under-eye fat for youthful charm (애교살)
- `inner-corner brightening` — white or champagne dot
- `monolid-flattering horizontal gradient` — pigment concentrated on the lash line, no faked crease

### Lash Vocabulary
- `individual lash clusters` — not strip lashes
- `wispy doll lashes`
- `spider-leg clumped lashes` — 60s mod look
- `bottom-lash extensions for doll-like wideness`
- `tightlined waterline` — invisible eyeliner inside the lash line

## 4. Lip Sculpting (唇部重塑)

### Reshaping Vocabulary
- `overlined cupid's bow to shorten the philtrum visually`
- `upward corner shading to lift the smile line`
- `inner-lip ombré — dark perimeter to plush center`
- `3D lip-gloss application — clear gloss only on the center plump`
- `lip-blurring with foundation around the edges` — modern soft-lip
- `pillowy 3D pout` — overdrawn lower lip for plump effect

### Texture Vocabulary
- `velvet matte lip` — pigment-rich, no shine
- `lacquered glossy lip` — wet, mirror-finish
- `stained lip flush` — sheer, blotted, blurred edges
- `glazed lip with concentrated center shine`

### Color-Direction Vocabulary
- `bold red statement lip` — true blue-red / orange-red / brick-red
- `nude my-lips-but-better` — one shade deeper than natural
- `berry-stained pout`
- `90s brown-toned lip with darker liner`
- `coral-flushed lip melted into the cheek blush`

## 5. Color & Focal Point (色彩與焦點)

### Palette Strategies
- `monochromatic palette` — same hue family on eyes, cheeks, lips (all warm peach; all dusty rose)
- `high-contrast focal point` — ONE saturated element (e.g., bold red lip) against muted everything else
- `analogous warm palette` — peach + coral + terracotta
- `cool-tone editorial palette` — mauve + plum + silver
- `sunburnt summer palette` — bronzed cheeks + glossy nude lip + warm-brown eye

### Focal Point Rules
- **One hero, two supports** — never compete bold eyes AND bold lips unless the brief is explicitly "maximalist."
- **Negative space matters** — muted areas frame the focal point.
- **Skin can be the hero** — sculpted-skin-forward looks treat lips and eyes as the muted supports.

### Flaw-as-Feature Vocabulary
- `freckles enhanced with subtle bronzer dotting across the nose bridge`
- `monolid celebrated with a horizontal smoky gradient` — no faked crease
- `gap-toothed smile left visible, no digital smoothing`
- `facial asymmetry preserved in the makeup mapping` — eyeliner traced to the actual eye shape, not symmetrized
- `birthmark or beauty mark intentionally darkened with a brow pencil`

## 6. Technique-to-Effect Cheat Sheet

| User Goal | English Technique Phrase |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| Look more awake | `inner-corner brightening + aegyo sal highlight + lifted outer wing` |
| Look more sculpted | `cool-toned cheek-hollow contour + liquid highlight on cheekbone arc + nose-bridge shading` |
| Look softer / younger | `cream blush on apples of cheeks + glossy lip center + wispy lower lashes` |
| Look editorial / bold | `cut-crease eyeshadow + overlined matte lip + sharp graphic liner` |
| Look natural but elevated | `tinted-moisturizer base + brushed-up brows + stained lip flush + cream bronzer` |
| Look powerful / cinematic | `chiseled contour + matte siren eye + lacquered bold lip` |
| Look ethereal / dewy | `glass-skin glow + monochromatic peach wash + glazed lip` |
| Preserve unique features | `freckles visible through sheer base + asymmetry-mapped eyeliner + natural brow shape` |

## 7. Lighting Pairings (Showcase the Work)

The lighting clause must be chosen to make the chosen technique READABLE. Wrong lighting erases the work.

| Makeup Hero | Best Lighting Phrase |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **Matte bold lip** | `soft frontal beauty light, slightly elevated, even illumination across the face` — frontal light keeps the lip color flat and saturated |
| **Cut crease / graphic eye** | `slightly elevated key light casting a delicate shadow into the crease, defining the lid sculpture` |
| **Sculpted skin (contour)** | `directional 45-degree key light from camera-left, soft fill on the right, ensuring the cheek hollow shadow reads clearly` |
| **Glass skin / dewy glow** | `soft diffused ring-style light with controlled specular highlights on the high planes` |
| **Freckles / flaw-as-feature** | `natural window light from a wide soft source, close to the subject — preserves skin texture without flattening it` |
| **Monochromatic palette** | `warm tungsten ambient at ~3200K` (for warm palettes) or `neutral daylight at ~5600K` (for cool palettes) — temperature reinforces palette |
| **High-contrast focal point** | `darker ambient with a single soft key on the focal feature, the rest gently in shadow` |

### Camera Framing Suggestions
- `tight beauty close-up framing, shoulders just visible, 85mm lens compression`
- `editorial half-body framing with the face centered in the upper third, fashion-magazine composition`
- `extreme macro on the focal feature (lip OR eye)` — only for ONE option in a set, not all three
