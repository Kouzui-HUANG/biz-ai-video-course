# Cinematic Vocabulary Reference

## Camera Distances
| Code | Term | Usage |
|------|------|-------|
| ECU | Extreme Close-Up | Micro-detail: iris, fingertip, texture |
| CU | Close-Up | Face fills frame |
| MCU | Medium Close-Up | Head and shoulders |
| MS | Medium Shot | Waist up |
| MLS | Medium Long Shot | Knees up |
| LS | Long Shot | Full body with environment |
| ELS | Extreme Long Shot | Subject small in vast environment |

## Camera Angles
| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Ground Level | Camera at ground surface |
| Low Angle | Camera below eye level, looking up |
| Eye Level | Camera at subject's eye height |
| High Angle | Camera above subject, looking down |
| Bird's-eye View | Directly overhead |
| Dutch Angle / Tilted | Camera rotated on roll axis for unease |
| Over-the-Shoulder (OTS) | Framed past another subject's shoulder |

## Micro-Detail ECU Targets (Mandatory 1-2 per grid)
Use these to create suspense or emotional tension by **completely omitting** the full face or body:

* **Eyes / Iris**: `Macro shot of the eyes/iris reflecting light`
* **Hands**: `Extreme close-up of hands clutching, pointing, or interacting with [object]`
* **Feet / Soles**: `Low angle extreme close-up focusing on the soles/footwear`
* **Held Prop / Key Object**: `Tight framing on a held prop or weapon, [describe object]`
* **Lips / Mouth**: `Extreme close-up of lips parting, whispering`
* **Fabric / Texture**: `Macro shot of fabric texture, stitching detail`

## Expression & Pose Modifiers
Vary these across panels to ensure each variant is distinct:

### Expressions
serene, intense stare, smirking, tearful, wide-eyed shock, melancholic gaze, clenched jaw, subtle smile, blank/emotionless, fierce determination

### Poses / Actions
arms crossed, leaning forward, turning away, hand reaching out, mid-stride, crouching, looking over shoulder, head tilted down, fist clenched at side, holding an object close to chest
