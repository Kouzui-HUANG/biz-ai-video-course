# Analysis Protocol — Detailed Reference

## Visual Deconstruction Dimensions

### A. Photography Composition (Spatial Framework)
Analyze the spatial layout of the image:

| Dimension        | What to identify                                                        |
|------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Camera distance  | Extreme close-up, Close-up, Medium close-up, Medium shot, Full shot, Establishing wide shot |
| Camera angle     | High angle, Low angle, Dutch tilt, Eye-level, Bird's eye, Worm's eye   |
| Depth of field   | Shallow depth of field / Bokeh, Deep focus, Selective focus             |
| Composition      | Rule of thirds, Leading lines, Symmetrical, Golden ratio, Centered     |

### B. Subject Details (Entity Characteristics)

- **Demographics**: Age representation, perceived gender.
- **Hair**: Style (bob, ponytail, twin-tails, etc.), length, precise color (e.g., "platinum blonde with lavender tips").
- **Eyes**: Shape (almond, round, tapered), color, reflections/highlights.
- **Attire**: Clothing type, fabric textures (adapted to 2D flat shading), accessories, brand/style cues, layering.
- **Kinesics**: Facial micro-expressions, dynamic posture, hand placement, line of action, weight distribution.

### C. Scene & Atmosphere (Environmental Context)

- **Setting**: Specific indoor/outdoor location, architectural elements, background props, spatial depth.
- **Lighting**: Light source direction and type:
  - Backlit, Rim lighting, Volumetric rays, Chiaroscuro
  - Soft diffused, Hard directional, Neon glow
- **Time & Weather**: Time of day (Golden hour, Blue hour, Twilight, Midnight), meteorological conditions (Foggy, Sun-drenched, Ethereal glow, Overcast, Rain-soaked).
- **Mood**: Overarching emotional tone (Melancholic, Euphoric, Serene, Tense, Nostalgic, Whimsical).

## Prompt Synthesis Guidelines

### Structural Sequence
Build the final prompt in this order:
1. Medium & artistic style declaration
2. Subject description (demographics, physical traits)
3. Attire & accessories
4. Action, pose, and expression
5. Camera composition & framing
6. Environment & setting details
7. Lighting, atmosphere, and mood

### Language Rules
- Write in highly descriptive, flowing **English**.
- Weave keywords into cohesive natural language paragraphs — **no comma-separated tag lists**.
- Use vivid, specific adjectives (e.g., "honeyed amber eyes" not just "amber eyes").
- Layer environmental details to create depth (foreground → midground → background).
- **Simplification & Stylization**: Actively compress complex physical details (e.g., tiling, dense foliage, metallic grilles) into broad, expressive color blocks and clean geometric shapes. Strictly avoid over-detailed linework and hyper-realistic features.
- **Facial De-realization (Anime Proportions)**: Forcefully reduce human facial realism. Translate facial structures into classic anime geometry: large, expressive, simplified eyes; a minimal nose (represented by a dot or soft line); a small, refined mouth; and a soft, smooth V-shaped jawline. Never describe realistic lips, nostrils, distinct cheekbones, or pronounced nasolabial folds.
- **No Specific IP References**: Do NOT reference specific animation studios (e.g., Studio Ghibli, Kyoto Animation) or directors (e.g., Makoto Shinkai) by name. Use pure visual terms like "flat cel-shading", "smooth contour lines", and "2D anime screencap".
